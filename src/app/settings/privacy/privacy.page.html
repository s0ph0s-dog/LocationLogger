Privacy Policy

I don't want your data. Data are like radioactive materials: they can be useful in specific ways, generally you shouldn't get much of them together in one spot, and if you aren't careful with them you'll hurt yourself or others.

This application is a Progressive Web Application, but it does not send any data that you enter to any server. All of the data you provide are stored exclusively on your device, using data storage features offered by your web browser (IndexedDB). You have complete control over your data, and can do with it as you please.

Right now, this application has no support for showing your locations on a map. I do not want to send your location data to Google if I can help it. I will see how possible it is to include an OpenStreetMap viewer, when I get around to adding the detail/edit functionality for log entries.

In the future, I may add a feature to send you a notification if you haven't logged your location by a specific time each day. Because browser makers are not interested in enabling time-based notification triggers for web applications, I will have to send a push notification from my web server. If I do choose to add this functionality, I will need to collect:

  • A push notification identifier for your device, and
  • The time of day at which you want to be notified.

These pieces of information will be used exclusively to send you reminder notifications. They will be deleted as soon as you turn off notifications, or after 3 days of failed notification deliveries (because you deleted the app, etc.).

If browser makers decide to enable time-based notification triggers, I will rip out all of the server notification functionality, and collect no information from you.
